Sussurro is a system-wide voice-to-text tool that runs entirely on your machine. Press a hotkey, speak, and your words appear as clean, formatted text in any application — no cloud, no subscriptions, no data leaving your device.

It uses **Whisper.cpp** for speech recognition and a fine-tuned **Qwen 3** language model to remove filler words, handle self-corrections, and produce readable output.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Press your hotkey">
    Hold `Ctrl+Shift+Space` (Linux) or `Cmd+Shift+Space` (macOS). The overlay capsule animates to show recording is active.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Speak naturally">
    Talk as you normally would — filler words, self-corrections, and stutters are handled automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Release to transcribe">
    Release the hotkey. Whisper converts your speech to text, and Qwen 3 cleans it up.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Text appears">
    The cleaned text is injected into whatever application is focused — no copy-paste needed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Key features

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Fully local" icon="lock">
    No data leaves your machine. Whisper.cpp and Qwen 3 run entirely offline.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Native overlay UI" icon="window">
    A pill-shaped capsule shows recording and transcribing state on Linux and macOS.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Smart cleanup" icon="wand-magic-sparkles">
    LLM removes filler words, fixes self-corrections, and prevents hallucinations.
  </Card>
  <Card title="System-wide injection" icon="keyboard">
    Works in any application where you can type — editors, browsers, terminals, chat apps.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Flexible hotkeys" icon="key">
    Push-to-talk or toggle mode. Change the hotkey live from Settings — no restart needed.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Wayland support" icon="linux">
    Full Wayland support via UNIX socket trigger server for GNOME, KDE, Sway, and Hyprland.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
